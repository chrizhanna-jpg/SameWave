## 1. Purpose

## 2. Confidential information

“**Confidential Information**” means non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other, including:

- Software, algorithms, matching logic, product designs, and roadmaps  
- Business plans, pricing, metrics, and user research  
- Technical documentation, credentials, and security practices  

It does **not** include information that the receiving Party can show:

- (a) was public when received or became public without breach;  
- (b) was already known without restriction;  
- (c) was independently developed without use of Confidential Information; or  
- (d) was rightfully received from a third party without restriction.

---

## 3. Obligations

The receiving Party will:

- Use Confidential Information only for the Purpose  
- Not disclose it to anyone except employees/contractors who need it and are bound to similar confidentiality  
- Protect it with at least the same care as its own confidential information (and no less than reasonable care)  
- Not reverse-engineer or attempt to extract trade secrets for a competing product  

---

## 4. Compelled disclosure

If law or court order requires disclosure, the receiving Party will (where lawful) give prompt notice and cooperate on protective orders.

---

## 5. Term

- **Confidentiality** continues for **3 years** from the Effective date for general confidential information.  
- **Trade secrets** (information that remains a trade secret under applicable law) stay protected for as long as it remains a trade secret.

---

## 6. Return / destruction

On request or when work ends, the receiving Party will stop using and delete or return Confidential Information, except one archival copy if required by law or automatic backup policy.

---

## 7. No license

No license to patents, copyrights, or trademarks is granted except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the Purpose.
